## Opponent Snapshot: Wasted Potential

Wasted Potential has lost seven of eight. The one they won was a 27-20 shootout over Soft Ballz — meaning the offense can show up when it wants to, and it has shown up against the same Soft Ballz lineup that swept Maple Tree in Week 1. Calling them a soft matchup is technically accurate but lazy. The opponent has a pulse.

Their offense, game by game, has scored: 12, 6, 22, 7, 27, 13, 7, 2. There is no clear identity at the plate. The 27 came against Soft Ballz, the 2 came against cacheouts last week, and the rest is league-average chaos. They are averaging exactly 12.0 runs scored per game on the season.

The bigger story is on defense. Wasted Potential has allowed 18 or more runs in six of their eight games. They are giving up 20.5 runs per game on the season, which is the worst defense in Blue Division — by a clear margin.

### Through Common Opponents

Both teams have already played Soft Ballz, Bullseyes, and Wasted Talent. Six games, same three defenses, same arithmetic. Here is the actual head-to-head through those games:

| | Maple Tree | Wasted Potential |
|---|---|---|
| Record | 1-5 | 1-5 |
| Runs scored | 74 | 87 |
| Runs allowed | 107 | 137 |
| Run differential | **-33** | **-50** |
| Per game scored | 12.3 | 14.5 |
| Per game allowed | 17.8 | 22.8 |

Wasted Potential's bats have actually produced more than Maple Tree's against the same three defenses — 14.5 runs a game to our 12.3. That detail matters because it means tonight is not a free pass. They can put runs on the board. The gap on the scoreboard tonight should come from the other end of the field: Wasted Potential has bled 30 more runs across the same six games. Five extra runs per game. That is the entire game.

The math: same record against the same teams. Different defense behind it. Tonight is the test of which one of those defenses shows up.

## The Stakes

Make no mistake: tonight is the season.

Lose two and Maple Tree drops to 1-7 — tied with Wasted Potential at the bottom of Blue Division, and the gap to the rest of the standings becomes a math problem we may not get to solve. Lose two and every remaining game has to be a heroic recovery. Win one and we are 2-6 with our best stretch still in front of us. Win two and we are 3-5, the offense has its swagger back, and the season has a heartbeat again.

There is no Week 1 Soft Ballz excuse tonight. There is no Game 1 Wasted Talent five-run inning hiding inside the box score. The opponent is winless across every measurement that matters. The bats are loud — eight hitters slugging 1.000 or better. The lineup is at full strength, thirteen deep, and over half of it is on a recent-form hot streak. The schedule has handed us this matchup specifically because the schedule has been watching too.

We do not need any heroics tonight. We do not need Tristan to hit three. We do not need Glove to drive in eight. We just need every guy in the order to do what his season line already says he does — then play seven clean innings of slowpitch without handing out free bases.

Two and oh. That is the assignment. That is the standard. Anybody coming to Boncosky Blue tonight expecting less is in the wrong dugout.

Let's go take it.
