## Opponent Snapshot: cacheouts (5-3)

Cacheouts has been the best example in the division of *winning close games when the math says you shouldn't.* Their season run differential is **negative one** — 122 runs scored, 123 runs allowed — and yet they are 5-3. Pythagorean expectation would put them somewhere around 4-4. They are running roughly one full win ahead of their underlying numbers, which is the exact opposite of what Maple Tree has been doing all season (and historically — five seasons of running *under* Pythagorean).

Looking at their box scores tells the story. **Five of cacheouts's eight games have been decided by two runs or fewer.** They beat No Dice 16-15 in Week 2. They beat Wasted Talent 26-24 in Week 3. They beat Wasted Potential 9-7 in Week 4. They lost to Bullseyes 24-23 last Wednesday and won the nightcap 14-13. This is a team that has trained itself to win the last inning of a tight game. That is the part of the sport Maple Tree has been mailing back unopened.

The one place Maple Tree has the cacheouts archetype beaten: **offensive ceiling.** Cacheouts has scored 18 or more once all season (the 18-2 vs Wasted Potential blowout). Maple Tree has scored 16+ four times in eight games and crossed 23 twice. When this lineup is right, it produces louder than cacheouts has produced. The question has never been whether the bats can show up. The question is whether the rest can.

## What The Data Says To Do Tonight

The analytics breakdown from earlier this week is doing the talking here:

- **Score 15.** Maple Tree has scored 15+ in four of eight games this season; over five seasons, this franchise wins 72 percent of games where it clears 15 runs. Hit that number and history says you win.
- **Extend innings.** Wins average 41.5 PAs per game. Losses average 33.2. Every extra trip through the order is worth roughly 2-3 runs of expected production. Foul off pitches. Take the borderline ones. Make cacheouts throw their fourth and fifth at-bat per hitter, not their third.
- **Contact over power.** Hits correlate r=+0.93 with runs scored. Home runs correlate r=+0.58. The team that strings four singles together wins more games than the team that hits one solo HR. Tristan does not need to put one over the fence tonight. He needs Glove and JJ to be on base when he does.
- **The defense needs ONE clean inning per game.** Cacheouts is a one-run-game specialist. The way you flip a close-game pattern is by playing the inning that turns a 13-13 game into a 13-9 lead. That is the math.
