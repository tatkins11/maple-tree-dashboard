# Week 1 Postgame Recap

## Weekly Result Summary
- Maple Tree opened the season 0-2 against Soft Ballz, dropping Game 1 by a 17-7 score and Game 2 by an 18-17 score.
- The record is ugly, but the offensive file is not: Maple Tree scored 24 runs, collected 35 hits, hit five home runs, and posted a .515/.547/.809 team slash with a 1.355 OPS.
- Game 1 was the reminder that slowpitch does not care about vibes once an inning gets loose. Game 2 was the reminder that scoring 17 runs usually solves most civic problems, unless the opponent saves a four-run final inning for dramatic municipal sabotage.
- The immediate takeaway is simple: the bats are ready. The next step is turning loud innings into complete games and preventing one defensive leak from becoming a full committee hearing.

## Game 1 Recap
- Result: Soft Ballz 17, Maple Tree 7.
- Setting: Wed 04/22/26 at 6:30 PM | Home | Boncosky Blue.
- Game flow: Maple Tree scored two in the first and had a late four-run fifth, but Soft Ballz stacked seven runs in the second and six more in the third. That 13-run middle-inning avalanche turned the opener from a normal softball game into an emergency budget meeting.
- Offensive notes: Glove drove in four, Tristan drove in three, and Duff reached twice while scoring two runs. Walsh, Joey, Joel, and Duff all chipped in hits, but the lineup never fully got the conveyor belt moving before the deficit became the main character.
- Turning point: The second and third innings decided the game. Once Soft Ballz started cashing every extra baserunner, Maple Tree was forced into comeback mode before the lineup had enough trips through the order to answer.

## Game 2 Recap
- Result: Soft Ballz 18, Maple Tree 17.
- Setting: Wed 04/22/26 at 7:30 PM | Away | Boncosky Blue.
- Game flow: Maple Tree answered the opener with 17 runs and 27 hits, scoring in six of seven innings. The offense put up 2, 2, 3, 3, 0, 4, and 3 by inning, which is exactly the kind of run distribution that usually gets filed under "manager sleeps peacefully."
- The problem was the ending: Maple Tree took a 17-14 lead into the bottom of the seventh, then Soft Ballz scored four to steal it 18-17. That is not a loss so much as a scheduled lesson in why slowpitch leads require constant supervision, like toddlers near an open refrigerator.
- Offensive notes: Jj went 4-for-5 with three runs and three RBI, Glove went 3-for-5 with three runs, Tristan reached all four times with two hits, two walks, three runs, and three RBI, Tim went 4-for-5, Kives went 4-for-5 with five RBI, and Corey went 3-for-3 with a walk and two runs. That is a deeply unserious amount of production for a game that somehow still ended in paperwork.
- Turning point: The bottom of the seventh. Maple Tree had done enough offensively to win, but the final defensive inning became the whole story.

## Week MVP
- Tristan is the Week 1 MVP. Three home runs, six RBI, five runs, and a .750 OBP is the kind of line that forces the scorekeeper to confirm the app is not hallucinating.
- Jj, Glove, and Kives all had legitimate cases, but Tristan supplied the most force and the most immediate "please stop doing that" energy for the opposing dugout.

## Where to Improve Next Week
- Close innings cleaner. Soft Ballz scored 13 runs across the second and third innings of Game 1, then scored four in the bottom of the seventh in Game 2. Those are the innings that decide whether a doubleheader feels like progress or a group therapy exercise.
- Protect late leads with boring defense. Maple Tree does not need spectacular hero work next week; it needs routine outs, clean throws, and the emotional maturity to make the other team earn every extra base.
- Keep the offensive approach from Game 2, but avoid the one empty inning becoming a turning point. When the lineup is producing 27 hits, the job is to keep applying pressure until the other dugout starts discussing whether softball is technically supposed to be recreational.
- Turn traffic into separation earlier. The bats produced enough to win the second game, but one more crooked number before the seventh could have changed the entire night.
